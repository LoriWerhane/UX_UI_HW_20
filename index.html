COATS FOR COLORADO

NON PROFIT WEBSITE CASE STUDY

My Role

This was a group project. My role was UX/UI Designer, Research, Wireframes, Prototyping.

Overview

Coats for Colorado, a local nonprofit organization had an outdated website that was not providing enough information to its primary user.

Time/Tools

Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Vecteezy (clip art), Upsplash (photography), G-Suite, Zoom

Three weeks

Challenge

How can we make Coats for Colorado a better resource for the more than 120 nonprofit network partners who distribute the coats.

RESEARCH

We reached out to the President of Dependable Cleaners/Coats for Colorado, Steven Tolz. He was happy to talk to us at length about his goals, wants, struggles and successes. We learned a lot about who visits his current website (those in need of a coat) and those he would prefer used his website as a tool to distribute coats (his team of over 120 non profit partners). He also expressed a desire to attract potential distribution partners to help connect the 47,000 coats he collects, cleans, and sorts each winter with the 120 non profits and their patrons.

Major takeaways from this valuable information exchange:

Current website needs updating.

Content needs to include "TOOLKITS" to support programming.

Opportunities for a partner would allow for expansion of programs.

Needs a "MIDDLEMAN" to help distribute coats to Nonprofit partners.

Coats for Colorado collects, cleans, distributes 47,000 coats each winter.

120+ Colorado nonprofits partner with them to distribute coats.

Primary user should be nonprofit partners.

Current users are people in need of a coat.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Coats for Colorado was designed to provide a platform where users could receive or distribute coats for those in need. How might we expand the audience and provide more detailed information so that the nonprofit partners are able to efficiently meet their distribution goals while reaching a wider net needy Coloradans.

WIREFRAMES FROM LOW TO HIGH FIDELITY

Our group all got super busy and started wireframing early in week two of the project. We created low-fidelity wireframes that would be ready for user testing quickly. We knew the website would be more impactful if information was organized better; and with more graphics explaining the processes. We created the main page and relevant secondary/tertiary pages so it could be tested thoroughly.
